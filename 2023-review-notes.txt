Hochul SOTS project: notes on 2023 review and proposed next steps

File-format preference:
- Going forward, if I make a reading memo for you, I will default to .txt rather than .md unless there is a reason not to.

What "Homes and Community Renewal" is:
- In New York State terms this is HCR, the main state housing/community-development umbrella.
- In our current 2023 file it is also being used as a default inferred lead agency for most housing items.
- That means some HCR assignments may be directionally right but still need item-level verification.

What the 2023 file currently is:
- It is a first-pass table-of-contents extraction, not a cleaned commitment tracker yet.
- It has 150 rows.
- It appears to include about 11 rows that are really section headers rather than commitments.
- It still has wrapped/truncated text issues and some overly generic coding values like "other" and "mixed."

Why metrics/targets are sparse:
- Most 2023 table-of-contents lines are not quantified at the title level.
- In the current first pass, only 9 of 150 rows are tagged quantified = yes.
- So blank metric_or_target is often a source limitation, not just a missed coding step.
- Many metrics would need to be pulled from the body text, budget documents, or later implementation materials.

Could some items be binary yes/no?
- Yes. A separate field for "binary evaluable" would be useful.
- Example values:
  - binary_evaluable: yes / no / mixed
  - binary_unit: established / issued / enacted / launched / funded / published / designated
- Best use case: items whose core question is whether the state created, authorized, issued, or launched something.
- Weak use case: items like "expand," "improve," "support," or "increase access," which usually need spectrum-based evaluation.

Examples that look clearly binary from titles:
- Establish a Commission on the Future of Health Care
- Establish A New Health Care Technology Capital Program
- Establish the Office of Semiconductor Expansion, Management and Integration
- Establish Minority and Women's Business Enterprises Certification Reciprocity
- Launch Mobile Veterans Service Centers
- Launch "One ID for New York State" to Improve Digital Access
- Authorize the City of New York to Legalize Basement Apartments
- Allow New York City to Lower Its Speed Limit

Aging-plan example:
- 2022 includes: "Establish a State Master Plan for Aging."
- The 2022 book says Hochul "will issue an Executive Order implementing a State Master Plan for Aging."
- That makes the 2022 commitment strongly binary at the first layer: was the order issued / was the plan established?
- The 2023 book refers back to it as already underway: "Last year, Governor Hochul directed the creation of the first-ever statewide Master Plan for Aging..."
- So across years, this is exactly the kind of commitment that works well as both:
  - a continuity tag, and
  - a binary implementation-status field.

Recommended next steps:
1. Clean 2023 before doing strict 2022-2023 overlap work.
2. Add a few new analytic fields rather than jumping straight to more years.
3. Then compare 2022 and 2023 for continuity, expansion, and repetition.
4. Only after that scale to later years.

Practical sequence I would use:
1. Clean the 2023 inventory.
   Remove header rows, fix wrapped titles, standardize agency names, and reduce overuse of "other" and "mixed."
2. Add analytic fields to both 2022 and 2023.
   Especially binary_evaluable and continuity-related fields.
3. Bring 2022 and 2023 into dialogue.
   Tag each 2023 item as new, continuation, expansion, or follow-on to a 2022 item.
4. Build a short crosswalk file.
   One row per matched pair or cluster, so recurring priorities are easy to inspect.
5. Then decide whether to deepen evidence for 2022-2023 or move on to 2024.

My recommendation:
- Do not jump to additional years yet.
- First make 2022 and 2023 analytically stronger, because they will define the coding system for everything that follows.
- The highest-value immediate move is: clean 2023 and add binary/continuity fields to both years.
